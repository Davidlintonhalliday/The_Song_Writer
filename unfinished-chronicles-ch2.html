The Unfinished Chronicles — Chapter 2

by David Linton-Halliday

The fire had long since burned low, leaving only a red shimmer in the coals. Sarah sat awake, tracing her father’s map under the dim glow of lantern light. The lines were crude but deliberate — three circles, each marked with a sigil she didn’t recognize. A soft rustle outside drew her eyes to the doorway. “You shouldn’t be here,” said a voice, quiet and sharp. She froze.
