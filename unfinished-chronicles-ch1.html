The Unfinished Chronicles — Chapter 1

by David Linton-Halliday

It was near dusk when Sarah found herself standing before the ruined archway. The wind that swept through the empty courtyard smelled faintly of rain and iron. Her father had spoken of this place only once — a whispered story about a key that could open more than doors, a key that could awaken memories buried by time itself. She touched the cold stone, tracing the weathered carvings with careful fingers. Somewhere deep inside, she could almost feel the hum of something waiting — old, patient, and watching.
