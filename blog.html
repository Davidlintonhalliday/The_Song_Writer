How I Built My Website for Pocket Change

Building The Song Writer website wasn’t about showing off tech skills — it was about proving you don’t need a big budget or a team of designers to create something beautiful and personal. I wanted a space that felt like a handmade studio notebook, where I could post songs, stories, and the odd rant about creative life in rural New South Wales.

Starting from Scratch (and Curiosity)

I began with nothing but a blank page and the idea that I could learn as I went. I opened a simple text editor and started typing HTML. No builders, no paid themes — just learning by doing. The more I wrote, the more I realized it’s like songwriting: one verse (or line of code) at a time.

Free Tools and Hosting

I used Netlify for free hosting and GitHub to store my files. Both are reliable, and once connected, updates are as easy as saving a file and letting the system redeploy. No ads, no hidden charges — just clean hosting that gives me full control over my site’s look and feel.

Learning the Basics of HTML & CSS

HTML defines structure — your bones. CSS defines style — your soul. Once I understood that, everything clicked. I learned how to set margins, colors, and shadows so my pages felt alive. Every update was another brushstroke in what became a digital self-portrait of my creative world.

Designing for Connection, Not Perfection

Many websites try too hard to impress. I wanted mine to feel warm and human. The fonts are clean, the colors soft, and the layout works well on mobile. It’s a space where people can listen to songs, read stories, and send feedback directly to me through Formspree — no logins or spam.

Keeping It Simple (and Cheap)

My total cost? Practically zero. - Domain name: about $20 a year. - Hosting: free with Netlify. - Design: done with heart and patience. And that’s the beauty of it — when you build your own site, you own it, you understand it, and you can change it anytime without paying anyone.

Final Thoughts

Every creative person deserves their own corner of the internet. Whether you write, sing, paint, or rant — build a place that’s yours. Don’t wait for permission or a perfect plan. Just start typing, save often, and hit publish. The rest will come naturally.

Built with HTML, coffee, and a stubborn refusal to give up.
